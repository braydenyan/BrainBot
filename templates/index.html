Intracranial hemorrhage is a life-threatening condition where bleeding occurs inside the skull, requiring immediate and specialized treatment. It accounts for about 10% of strokes in the U.S., making rapid diagnosis crucial for effective intervention. Typically, specialists must urgently analyze medical images to locate and identify the type of hemorrhage, a process that is both time-consuming and complex. To expedite this critical diagnostic step, we have developed an algorithm designed to quickly detect acute intracranial hemorrhages and their subtypes. This innovation aims to reduce the time needed for diagnosis, thereby potentially improving patient outcomes.
